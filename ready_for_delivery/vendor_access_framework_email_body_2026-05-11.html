Working assumptions

These are the working assumptions.

  1. A third Spark cluster (msb-pmc04) will be added alongside the existing dev cluster (msb-pmc03) and production cluster (msb-pmc02).
  2. msb-pmc04 hosts: Airflow + ancillary services (Grafana / Prometheus / Loki, Bastion VM, Ansible playbook source). The msb-pmc01 orchestration cluster is being retired — its services migrate to msb-pmc04; once migration completes, msb-pmc01 is out of the Spark fabric.
  3. VLAN 37 is the unified Spark Cluster Network — spans all three Spark clusters (msb-pmc02, msb-pmc03, msb-pmc04) and the VMs running on them. Every Spark-related service lives in VLAN 37.
  4. Vendor (Ksolves) needs access to all three Spark clusters during setup and configuration. Dev ↔ Prod isolation is explicitly not in scope at this design stage. Facilitating vendor access across all three clusters is the priority; tighter dev/prod posture is deferred.
  5. Vendor authenticates via AD when logging into the fqdn Horizon VDI pool (already stood up 2026-05-07 — ks_test AD group attached, UAG-reachable). Vendor accounts and groups are provisioned (as of 2026-05-11).
  6. From the VDI, vendor sees VLAN 37 only as Spark-related network surface. Thus the rest of the corporate network is invisible at the L2 layer; reach to enterprise services (AD, DNS) is mediated by Austin's firewall.

Primary control: Austin's network firewall.

  • Single chokepoint at the pool's default gateway (confirmed 2026-05-08)
  • Allow / deny policy as enumerated in the diagram above

Secondary control: Defense-in-depth — host-level firewalls on every VLAN 37 host.

  • nftables on Proxmox hosts (Debian); firewalld on RHEL 9.4 VMs
  • Outbound default-deny; allow VLAN 37 internal + AD DCs + DNS
  • Vendor sudo carve-out disallows nft / iptables / ufw / firewall-cmd so the host-layer rules can't be lifted operationally
  • Simplified from Paul Barber's 2026-05-06 mitigation proposal — the primary control already filters at the firewall, so the host rules are a backstop rather than the only line

VLAN 37 Sole Tenancy

Under this framework, VLAN 37 is vendor- and Spark-services-only.

  • No other fqdn workload tenants share VLAN 37
  • With msb-pmc01 being retired (item 2 in Working assumptions), VLAN 37 becomes structurally sole-tenanted by the three Spark clusters as the retirement completes
  • VLAN membership becomes the access boundary at the L2 layer, with Austin's firewall + host firewalls as the policy controls layered on top

IP space sizing: Three clusters × ~3 hosts each + their VMs is comfortably inside a /24 (254 host addresses). If host count or VM density outgrows the envelope later, VLAN 37 can be expressed as a /23 or larger contiguous block.

Enterprise Services in Scope (vendor needs reach)

Vendor reach to these is explicit — not implied by VLAN 37 membership. Austin's firewall is the enforcement point.
